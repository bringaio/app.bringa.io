# Hyperoptimum

Hyperoptimum is the project's guiding quality ideal. It does not mean maximum abstraction, maximum feature count, or constant refactoring. It means the best reachable balance across people, code, data, operations, forks, agents, and time.

Ultraoptimum is the longer-horizon pull behind that ideal: the repository should keep becoming clearer, safer, more humane, more reliable, and easier to extend without confusing aspiration with urgency.

## What It Means Here

- Maintainability: future contributors can understand the system quickly.
- Scalability: data, workflows, and governance can grow without hidden rewrites.
- Extensibility: forks and deployments have stable extension points.
- Testability: important behavior can be verified without fragile rituals.
- Consistency: similar things are named, shaped, and documented similarly.
- Reusability: shared config, components, docs, skills, and workflows are useful across forks.
- Accessibility: core flows work for keyboard, screen reader, mobile, and low-bandwidth users.
- Performance: images, fonts, client code, and builds stay lean.
- Reliability: failure modes, backups, and recovery paths are explicit.
- Security: RLS, secrets, uploads, admin actions, and privacy boundaries are designed defensively.
- Observability: maintainers can diagnose problems without exposing user data.
- Developer experience: checks are fast, docs are compact, and the next correct step is obvious.

## What It Feels Like

For developers and agents, the target feeling is calm confidence: the repository is small enough to reason about, structured enough to extend, and honest enough that warnings, TODOs, docs, and tests tell the truth.

For fork operators, the target feeling is dignity and control: local legal text, branding, language, Supabase setup, and operations can diverge without making upstream collaboration painful.

For users, the target feeling is trust: the app treats their identity, images, locations, borrowing history, and contributions with care.

## How Agents Should Apply It

- Start with the smallest coherent step that improves the whole system.
- Prefer source-of-truth clarity over repeated prose or scattered constants.
- Record valuable out-of-scope ideas in `docs/optimization-options.md`.
- If an idea has uncertain impact, possible side effects, or needs current research, say so in the entry.
- Do not use "hyperoptimum" as permission to expand scope. Use it as pressure toward clarity, restraint, verification, and long-term coherence.
